## Overview

The Prism is an ergonomic leverless controller released in November 2021 by WunVzn. The retail price was $450 USD.

The Prism is built for platform fighter players who want an ergonomic alternative to the GameCube controller. Its layout is inspired by the Frame1, but with the action clusters rotated distally along the roll axis, inward on the yaw axis, and forward in pitch, eliminating the need for forearm pronation and ulnar deviation. The idle stance of the fingers rests in a naturally curled position, and the tight distance between the directional buttons allows the thumb to trigger the five-button action cluster with minimal movement.

## Development

### MK1

The case was constructed from titanium panels. The MK1 featured 19 directional buttons.

### MK3

The MK3 was the first Prism with a fully 3D printed enclosure and a custom PCB. The 3D printing opened up color customization options for end users, including front panel logos and inlay logos.

### MK4

The MK4 introduced significant hardware improvements:
- A USB-C combo port
- 8 action keys (up from 5)
- A Brook Board header.

### MK6

The MK6 is the final production model.

## Technical Specifications

### Switch Configuration

**Stock Switches**: Kailh Speed Silver (left/directional side) with 4 mm O-rings; Gateron Clear (right/action side) with 2 mm O-rings.
